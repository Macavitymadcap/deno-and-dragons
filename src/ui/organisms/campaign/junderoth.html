An Overview of Junderoth

History

Junderoth appeared on Qor'thos only 2 years ago. Where it came from scholars argue over, but what they do agree upon is that the place is brimming with magic. The Prismatic Reef on it's eastern side bedazzled many sailors when they enocuntered it, and a strange white cloud of mirages visible over the landmass havevalso been reported.

The first nation to take an interest was Bay-Tsao, followed by Käs. Both being to the west of Junderoth they managed to easily navigate the Misty Archipelago and establish trade routes with the Tideweaver Islanders. The Jade Throne has extended it's reach by pushing into the interior with their eyes on the wonders and resources of the Sunspire Mountains.

Käs has been more interested in the Obsidian Valleys and the Thundering Plains, establishing trade routes with the Obsidian League and the Thunderbird Nomads. They have no great colonial ambition, being more interested in the acquisition and trade of resources. While their aggressive trade policies have made them enemies with the Sunspire Confederation.

The Emerald Canopy

Vast rainforest covering much of central Junderoth, contains hidden ruins of ancient civilizations. Its is home to the Rootbond Collective and countless magical creatures

Cultures

The Rootbond Collective

A diverse range of peoples and creatures who live in harmony with the rainforest, dwelling in living treehouses. They practice plant-based magic and herbal medicine. Governed by a council of elders chosen by the forest itself.

Flora

Fauna

Jade Jaguars

Big cats with crystalline fur that can blend into jungles. Considered sacred by the Rootbond Collective.

The Misty Archipelago

Chain of volcanic islands off the western coast of the main contenent. Each island has unique magical properties, and it is the domain of the Tideweaver Islanders.

Cultures

The Tideweaver Islanders

Expert navigators and seafarers whose society is structured around extended family units (whanau).

Fauna

Bunyip

Shapeshifting water creatures. Can be befriended by skilled Tideweaver Islanders.

Taniwha

Guardian creatures of the sea and rivers. Can be benevolent protectors or fearsome monsters.

The Sunspire Mountains

Towering mountain range in the north whose peaks often shrouded in magical auroras. Home to the Sunspire Confederation's pyramid-cities.

Cultures

The Sunspire Confederation

Sun-worshipping society with complex calendar system who live in pyramid-cities built atop mountains. They are skilled astronomers and mathematicians.

Flora

Fauna

Quetzldrake

Feathered dragon-like creatures with rainbow plumage. Revered by the Sunspire Confederation.

The Thundering Plains

Expansive grasslands in the east that is frequent plagued with arcane storms that reshape the landscape. It is roaming grounds of the Thunderbird Nomads.

Cultures

The Thunderbird Nomads

Nomads following herds of magical beasts who revere giant, magical birds and use their feathers in rituals. They are skilled shamans who can communicate with spirits.

Flora

Fauna

Tikbalang

Horse-headed humanoids that can run at incredible speeds. Known to lead travelers astray in the Thundering Plains.

Thunderbirds

Enormous, electricity-generating birds. Feathers are used in powerful magical rituals.

The Obsidian Valleys

A network of mountain valleys in the south that teams with minerals, ores and other resources, both natural and supernatural. It is the stronghold of the Obsidian League.

Cultures

The Obsidian League

These dwarf master stoneworkers and metallurgists build intricate cities in mountain valleys connected by magical ley lines. They worship the mountains and the minerals within them

Flora

Fauna

Axex

Giant, armored armadillo-like creatures. Used by the Obsidian League for mining and construction.

The Prismatic Reef

The colours of this massive, magical coral reef off the eastern coast of Junderoth shift and change constantly, throwing off spellcasting and navigation. Its depths are home to merfolk and other aquatic races.

Whispering Desert

A mysterious desert in the northwest dotted with mirages that are gateways to other planes. Sand dunes that sing in magical harmonies.

Port Walen

Port Walen is a fledgling outpost on the coast of Junderoth, barely a few years old. It's a mix of hastily constructed buildings, tents, and some sturdier structures, sprawling from a natural cove inland. The town is surrounded by a wooden palisade, constantly being expanded as the settlement grows.

The air in Port Walen is thick with anticipation and tension. The smell of salt water mixes with the earthy scent of the nearby forest. The constant sound of construction and the shouts of workers and guards create a bustling atmosphere. Despite attempts at order, there's an underlying sense of lawlessness and opportunity that permeates the settlement.

The Docks

Rickety wooden structures extending into the cove. Always busy with ships loading/unloading supplies and prisoners. Warehouses nearby storing goods and equipment.

Governor's Mansion

The only stone building in town, doubling as an administrative center. Houses Governor Elara Brightstone and her staff. Contains a small jail in the basement.

The Rusty Anchor Tavern

Popular gathering spot for off-duty guards and workers. Run by a retired sailor, serves as informal information hub.

Marketplace

Open-air market where traders sell goods from the Old World and Junderoth. Mix of permanent stalls and temporary setups.

Prisoner Barracks

Series of long, wooden buildings where transported criminals are housed. Surrounded by its own fence and guard posts.

Expedition Outfitters

Shop selling supplies for forest expeditions and mining operations. Run by a knowledgeable but eccentric gnome.

Arcanium Workshop

Where Dr. Lydia Thornwind conducts her research. Filled with strange specimens and magical apparatus.

The Clearing Yards

Area at the edge of town where forest clearing operations are organized. Noisy with the sound of axes and arcanopunk logging equipment.

Shantytown

Sprawling area of tents and makeshift shelters. Houses many of the workers and some reformed prisoners.

Watchtowers

Wooden structures spaced along the palisade. Manned by guards keeping an eye on both the forest and the town.

Flora

Cloudfruit Trees

Massive trees that produce fluffy, edible clouds. Eating the fruit grants temporary levitation.

Chroma Orchids

Flowers that change color based on nearby emotions. Used in emotion-manipulating potions.

Walking Palms

Trees that can slowly move to find better soil or sunlight. Leaves can be brewed into a potion of plant control.

Lumina Vines

Bioluminescent vines that react to magical energies. Used to detect and measure magical fields.

Ironwood Trees

Trees with bark as hard as Ironwood is prized for crafting magical staves and wands.

Whisperweed

Grass that records and repeats snippets of conversation. Used for spy work and preserving oral histories.

Timefruit Bushes

Produce fruit that ages or youthen those who eat it. Highly regulated and protected by all cultures.

Fauna

Ahuizotl

Dog-like water monsters with hand-tipped tails. Known to lure prey to watery dooms

Living Totem Poles

Sentient, mobile wooden creatures. Carved with the histories of the land.

Menehune

Small, dwarf-like creatures with incredible strength. Master craftsmen who work only at night.
